# أمان مستدام — Sustainable Safety Initiative

## What it is

A national strategic initiative authored by Ahmad Al-Shahri to establish and institutionalise a **comprehensive disability-inclusive emergency and disaster management framework** across Saudi rehabilitation and social-care facilities.

**Formal title** (JSONL #18):
> *المبادرة الوطنية الاستراتيجية لتأسيس وتعميم منظومة إدارة الطوارئ والكوارث الشاملة للأشخاص ذوي الإعاقة في مراكز التأهيل والرعاية وما في حكمها.*

**Stakeholder-deck framing** (JSONL #42, file 50):
> *نحو ريادة استباقية في الجاهزية الشاملة: استراتيجية وطنية لسلامة وتمكين الأشخاص ذوي الإعاقة في الطوارئ والأزمات.*

**Synonym handling.** *أمان مستدام* and *السلامة المستدامة* appear interchangeably in the working Stitch materials (JSONL #23, "أو السلامة المستدامة"). Canonical form for vault retrieval: **أمان مستدام**; *السلامة المستدامة* is registered as an alias.

**Status: active.** The Al-Baha implementation tender was awarded (*ترسية*) at **SAR 6,261,779** with a **12-month execution window** — figures confirmed across JSONL #3 and #7. Aman Mustadam is one of four named Ahmad-authored strategic initiatives in [[research-initiatives-portfolio]], alongside *تمكين* (Pay-for-Success), the satisfaction-measurement system (*صوتك مسموع* / Smart Mood Bracelet), and *الإحسان قبل المعيار*.

**Patron / authorisation.** The proposed governance structure places the initiative under a *اللجنة الوطنية العليا للمبادرة* chaired by the Minister, with a PMO beneath it (JSONL #18). Stakeholder-engagement targets are senior MHRSD leadership, *هيئة رعاية الأشخاص ذوي الإعاقة* (APD), and *الدفاع المدني* (JSONL #42 file 52). Per the wider vault graph, [[ismail-alghamdi]] is the ministry-level patron route; [[nasser-alqahtani]] is the formal domain owner. **Both patronage links are inferred from the portfolio stakeholder map, not stated verbatim in the Aman Mustadam documents themselves.**

## CRPD Article 11 anchor + the 2-4× claim

**Primary international legal basis:** Article 11 of the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (situations of risk and humanitarian emergencies), cited explicitly in JSONL #18, #23, #42 (file 53).

**Domestic counterpart:** *المادة 27 من النظام الأساسي للحكم* —
> *تكفل الدولة حق المواطن وأسرته، في حالة الطوارئ، والمرض، والعجز، والشيخوخة.*
(JSONL #18, #23 file 13/9.)

**The mortality-multiplier claim.** Stated verbatim in the initiative's rationale:
> *معدل وفيات الأشخاص ذوي الإعاقة خلال الكوارث يزيد بمقدار 2-4 أضعاف مقارنة بغيرهم.*
(JSONL #18.)

⚠️ Upstream citation for the 2-4× statistic is not surfaced in the JSONL summaries. **Evidenced inside the initiative documents; primary source unverified** — typical sources for this kind of claim would be UN OHCHR / WHO / academic disaster-research literature. Verify before propagating the statistic outside the initiative context.

## Initiative architecture — four-phase disaster cycle

Each phase tailored for disability inclusion (JSONL #18 is the richest source):

### 1. الوقاية والتخفيف (Mitigation)

- Strict application of **Saudi Building Code (SBC 201 structural, SBC 801 accessibility)** (JSONL #23 file 6).
- *مناطق ملاذ آمن* (Areas of Refuge) on upper floors.
- Multi-sensory alarm systems — audio + visual + vibration — covering hearing- and vision-impaired residents.
- Engineered evacuation devices: **stairway evacuation chairs** replacing manual lifting.

### 2. الاستعداد والجاهزية (Preparedness)

- **Personal Emergency Evacuation Plans (PEEPs)** per resident — individualised by type of assistance, essential equipment, preferred routes. Cross-link: PEEPs framework already documented in [[999-albaha-qms-bcp]].
- Training under **ICS / HICS** (Incident Command System / Hospital Incident Command System).
- Drill cadence: tabletop, functional, full-scale.
- Equipment: early-warning multi-modal systems, tactile paving, backup generators.

### 3. الاستجابة (Response)

- Activated **EOC** (Emergency Operations Centre).
- Staged evacuation with PEEP activation; triage by proximity to hazard.
- External notification: **Civil Defence (998), Red Crescent (997)** — transmitting facility maps + disability-type roster + resident locations.
- Accessible communication tools at assembly points: communication boards, sign-language interpreters.

### 4. التعافي (Recovery)

- Damage + structural-safety assessment.
- Disability-appropriate alternative shelters.
- Long-duration **MHPSS** (Mental Health and Psychosocial Support) for residents, staff, and families.
- Formal debriefing with lessons-learned capture and plan updates.
- Diffusion of best practice to peer centres.

## Pilot at Al-Baha — three-stage geographic ladder

Per JSONL #23 (Stitch folder):

- **Stage 1 — Pilot (Year 1–2).** Al-Baha centre as *نموذج ريادي*.
- **Stage 2 — Development (Year 2–3).** Build the national reference manual to be generalised.
- **Stage 3 — Scaling (Year 3–5).** Kingdom-wide generalisation across all government + private + day-care + specialised centres.

### Al-Baha 18-month pilot roadmap (JSONL #23, files 8/11)

- **Months 1–3**: risk + capability assessment, detailed action plan.
- **Months 4–6**: emergency-management framework + customised evacuation protocols.
- **Months 7–12**: training (basic + advanced) + equipment installation + early-warning system.
- **Months 13–18**: tabletop and functional drills, family-communication plans.
- **Continuous**: annual full-scale drill + PDCA updates.

**Why Al-Baha as pilot.** Ahmad's home base; institutional access. Al-Baha risk profile (JSONL #23): medium-grade earthquake exposure, flash floods, sandstorms; human-caused risks including armed-conflict proximity (geographic) and service disruption. Resident profile: majority cannot self-evacuate, rely on assistive devices, have communication challenges and continuous medical needs.

**Success metrics.** Annex 9 is the KPI container; specific numeric targets (evacuation-time benchmarks, drill-pass rates, PEEP coverage %) are **not surfaced** in the JSONL summaries — open question.
