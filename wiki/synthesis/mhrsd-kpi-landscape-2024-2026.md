# MHRSD KPI Landscape 2024–2026

## Why this map exists

By 2026-05-06 the vault held seven distinct KPI / quality artifacts for one rehabilitation center. They are:

| # | Artifact | Year | Layer | Owner | Wiki |
|--:|---|--:|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2025 KPI Portfolio framework + ministerial circular | 2025 | Medical-side scorecard | Ministry → center director | [[mhrsd-2025-kpi-portfolio]] |
| 2 | 2025 Master scorecard (8-dept × 100%) | 2025 | Medical-side scorecard | Center director | [[mhrsd-2025-kpi-master-scorecard]] |
| 3 | 2024 social-section templates (5 domains × 14 sub-KPIs) | 2024–2025 | Social-section parallel track | Social-services head | [[mhrsd-2024-kpi-social-section]] |
| 4 | 2026 health KPIs for social-care homes (43 indicators) | 2026 | Beneficiary-safety dashboard | Health supervisor (per-home) | [[mhrsd-2026-kpi-health-social-homes]] |
| 5 | Contractor SLA scorecard (1000-point) | 2024+ | Vendor evaluation | Branch committee | [[mhrsd-contractor-performance-scorecard]] |
| 6 | Branch quality unit operational plan (19 admin departments) | 2025 | Branch-level oversight | Branch GM + quality officer | [[mhrsd-2025-albaha-branch-quality-plan]] |
| 7 | Q3 facilities dashboard (42 indicators × 3 quarters) | 2025 | Facilities compliance | Center general-facilities admin | [[mhrsd-2025-albaha-q3-facilities-dashboard]] |

They share no common rollup. Read in isolation each is internally coherent; read together a structural picture emerges.

## The five layers

```
Layer 1 — Ministry portfolio framework
   تعميم 28597 (Jan 2025) → kingdom-wide standard for 36 rehab centers
   ↓
Layer 2 — Medical-side scorecard (100% weighted, additive)
   8 departments: Medical 30 / Nursing 25 / Pharmacy 12 / PT 6 / Speech 6 / Psych 8 / Nutrition 8 / Dental 5
   → director-level evaluation
   ↓ (parallel, NOT a sub-bucket)
Layer 2b — Social-section parallel track
   5 domains × 14 sub-KPIs: skill-dev, programs, vocational, family-guidance, after-care
   → social-services-head accountability
   ↓
Layer 3 — Beneficiary-safety dashboard (43 indicators, flat)
   Health-perf KPIs scoped to social-care homes; outcome-tracking introduced for 2026
   → health supervisor per home
   ↓
Layer 4 — Vendor SLA scorecard (1000 points)
   Contractor for cleaning + personal-care + laundry/kitchen + maintenance
   < 90% triggers improvement-plan obligation
   → branch committee + contractor representative
   ↓
Layer 5 — Branch + facilities oversight
   Branch quality unit covers all 19 administrative departments
   Q3 facilities dashboard tracks 42 accessibility/safety indicators per quarter
   → branch GM + named quality officer (Mariya Al-Mohammedi)
```

## Unit of analysis matters

The single biggest source of confusion when reading these artifacts is **which entity is being scored**:

| Artifact | Scored entity |
|---|---|
| Master scorecard | Comprehensive rehab center, by department |
| Social-section templates | Social-services department of one rehab center |
| 2026 social-homes KPIs | A *social-care home* (different facility class) |
| Contractor scorecard | An outsourced *vendor*, monthly |
| Branch quality plan | The *regional ministry branch* (covers all 19 departments) |
| Facilities dashboard | The *facilities department* of one rehab center |

The same beneficiary appears under multiple lenses depending on which artifact's window is open. **No single instrument gives a unified beneficiary-health view.**

## Empowerment vs medical-model framing

The **medical-side scorecard** (Layer 2) is dominated by clinical-throughput metrics with three structural anti-patterns documented in [[mhrsd-2025-kpi-master-scorecard]]:

1. Zero-event under-reporting incentive (3 of 7 medical KPIs reward absence-of-event)
2. Medical-model framing (no dignity / community-integration metric)
3. No beneficiary self-report

The **social-section track** (Layer 2b) inverts these:

| Critique | Medical-side | Social-section |
|---|---|---|
| Zero-event under-reporting | 3 of 7 medical KPIs | **None** — all 14 sub-KPIs positive-polarity |
| Medical-model framing | Dominant | **Inverted** — empowerment + community focus |
| No beneficiary self-report | True | **Partially addressed** — sub-KPI 5.2 (satisfaction) is the only self-report metric in the entire 2024–2026 inventory |

The social section also contains the **deinstitutionalization metric** (4.3: "% beneficiaries transitioned from rehab center to community services"), a rare pure-empowerment indicator inside an institutional KPI surface.

**The empowerment-aligned infrastructure already exists**. The strategic question is not "create new KPIs" but "shift weight + visibility from medical-side to social-section."

## The 2026 trajectory

The 2026 social-homes instrument ([[mhrsd-2026-kpi-health-social-homes]]) introduces three signals worth noting:

1. **Outcome metrics** appear for the first time — contractures, mobility-improvement, behavior-improvement, communication-improvement (all "vs prior assessment"). The 2025 scorecard had only process metrics on PT/Speech/Psych.
2. **"للقياس فقط" (measure-only) targets** — 4 indicators have no scoring threshold, only data collection. Honest signaling that the ministry is monitoring before deciding targets.
3. **Improvement-plan recursion** — improvement plans for under-performing KPIs are themselves a KPI (target 100%). The instrument *measures its own response loop*.

These three signals are policy-level alignment with the empowerment thesis Basira advances. **The ministry is moving in the same direction Basira pitches** — slower, with different vocabulary, but the same direction.

## Five gaps as Basira's product-market fit

The map exposes five concrete gaps a digital-transformation product can fill:

1. **No unified center-health view.** Five+ instruments, no rollup. → Aggregate dashboard.
2. **Honest "تقديرية" (estimated) values** in the Q3 facilities dashboard's quantitative side. → Real measurement infrastructure (sensors + log integration).
3. **Per-domain tracking logs (سجلات المتابعة)** are the upstream evidence base for the contractor scorecard. → Digital log-discipline = scorecard defensibility.
4. **Status-vs-notes inconsistency** (e.g., "applied + scheduled for Q4") in the Q3 dashboard. → Data-integrity layer that won't accept self-contradictory states.
5. **15-card ministry-liaison map** ([[mhrsd-2025-social-operational-plan-albaha]]) is paper-based escalation routing. → Digital escalation routing with SLA tracking.

Each gap is operationalizable, defensible against ministry priorities, and aligned with the customer-empowerment / service-experience anchor of the branch quality unit ([[mhrsd-2025-albaha-branch-quality-plan]]).
