# Overtime / Suspension / Resignation Grievance Case

> ⚠️ **Sensitivity flag.** This synthesis is litigation material. Per [[drive-vault-coverage-audit]] §4 it is treated with the same governance rigour as Basira-decisions material. Allegations are attributed to their source documents (Ahmad's escalation memos, the Nazaha dossier draft, the JSONL summaries) — not asserted as neutral findings. Status as of 2026-04-25 is unverified for several open items in §10.

## The case in one paragraph

An administrative-law dispute between **Ahmad bin Abdullah Al-Shahri** (employee no. 23200, PT specialist + quality officer + infection-control auditor at مركز التأهيل الشامل بالباحة) and the Al-Baha regional branch of HRSD, centered on the 2024 reapplication of a 2021 schedule-circular fixing an 8-hour working day on the health cadre — including Ahmad — in place of the 7-hour day mandated by Article 11 of the Executive Regulation of the Civil Service Human Resources (اللائحة التنفيذية للموارد البشرية).

Ahmad's position, documented across JSONL #20, #39, and #49, is threefold:

- The schedule decision was issued by **non-competent parties** (the health coordinator and the centre director) without the Ministerial delegation required by Articles 12 and 13 of the Executive Regulation.
- Enforcement via the biometric attendance system (نظام البصمة), tied to automatic salary deduction, constitutes **administrative coercion** rather than legitimate supervision.
- The internal handling of his grievance was **procedurally defective** — referral to operational units rather than the legal department.

The case has branched into three parallel tracks: (i) an internal escalation memo to the regional general manager, (ii) a planned administrative-court suit (ديوان المظالم) once the statutory 60-day silence period matured into an implicit rejection (رفض ضمني) on or around **18 September 2025**, and (iii) a Nazaha (نزاهة) complaint alleging moral forgery (التزوير المعنوي), misuse of authority, and administrative coercion. Per JSONL #20 the case sits at the *"legally ripe"* (ناضجة قانونياً) stage as of the source-summary date — escalation exhausted, court filing prepared, Nazaha dossier drafted. Court acceptance, Nazaha intake, and the documented status of the resignation/exemption request (طلب الإعفاء) are unverified pending direct confirmation with Ahmad.

## The legal vocabulary used

The four canonical phrases in [[empowerment-vocabulary]] (Procedural / Grievance section) apply to this case as follows. The phrases are **referenced by wikilink** rather than re-defined here.

- *اغتصاب صلاحية الوزير* (in [[empowerment-vocabulary]]). Applied in JSONL #20 and #49 to the act of issuing the 8-hour schedule at centre-director / health-coordinator level, in the absence of Ministerial delegation under Articles 12–13. Framed legally as *عدم الاختصاص الجسيم* (ultra vires; gross want of jurisdiction).
- *التزوير المعنوي* (in [[empowerment-vocabulary]]). Applied in JSONL #49 to the use of the *جدول دوام* to reconfigure the biometric system. The claim is not material falsification of a document; it is that an instrument lacking the formal elements of an administrative decision (no outgoing number, no seal, non-competent issuer) was used to produce a legal effect (changing recorded hours, triggering salary deduction) — meeting the definition under the penal system for forgery offences.
- *الإكراه الإداري* (in [[empowerment-vocabulary]]). Applied in JSONL #49 to the coupling of the schedule with automated salary deduction via the biometric system. Characterised as administrative coercion because non-compliance is penalised through automatic financial loss rather than a formal disciplinary process.
- *الكيل بمكيالين* (in [[empowerment-vocabulary]]). Applied in JSONL #20 and #49 to the selective application of health-cadre conditions: the administration imposes the 8-hour working day (a health-sector burden) while withholding *بدل العدوى* (infection allowance) and *بدل الإشراف* (supervision allowance) the same comparison would entitle Ahmad to. His formal assignment as *مدقق مكافحة عدوى* is cited as evidence the exposure basis for *بدل العدوى* is factually present.

## Quality-work / grievance-arc connection

## The #881/1446H distinction

Court appeal **case no. 881/1446 H**, referenced inside the F-2.1 [[al-baha-quality-project-2024]] folder and in JSONL #14, is a **separate matter** concerning **عبدالله بن سعيد الغامدي** — a social-services specialist at the same centre, contesting his performance-appraisal outcome. The appellant in that case is HRSD (Al-Baha branch) against the employee, before the Administrative Appeals Court in Asir region. The underlying first-instance case is **no. 1420/1445 H**.

This case is **NOT** Ahmad's overtime case. The co-location of the two cases in the same dossier indicates Ahmad cited the Al-Ghamdi appeal as **procedural precedent** (same regional branch, same employer-defendant posture, same administrative-court venue) — not as part of his own file. Future synthesis or inference work must preserve this distinction; conflating the two would misattribute facts, names, and outcomes. JSONL #14 also notes a third unrelated file — the case of *عبدالله الخمري* against the Ministry — likewise distinct from both.
