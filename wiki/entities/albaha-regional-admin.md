# Al-Baha Regional General Administration — HRSD

Dictated by Ahmad on 2026-04-23 while preparing Document B (the regional admin's strategic pitch). Use as the authoritative reference for any correspondence or planning that concerns the Al-Baha regional branch of HRSD.

## Top of hierarchy

**المدير العامّ (Director General)** — [[khalid-alzahrani]] (خالد بن صالح الزهراني, kunya: أبو فيصل). Step 0 for Basira institutional escalation per the Regional-GM governance rule.

2. **مساعد المدير العامّ لقطاع العمل** (Assistant DG for Labor Sector)
3. **مدير الخدمات المساندة** (Director of Support Services)

## Facilities under the Regional Admin

Not all are under direct line management, but all are part of the regional network:

- **مركز التأهيل الشامل بمنطقة الباحة** — see [[albaha-center-org]].
- Labour offices in Al-Baha governorates (محافظات: الباحة، المندق، قلوة، المخواة، العقيق، بلجرشي، القرى، الحجرة).
- Social security offices.
- Social development units.

## Why this matters

1. **Document B (Regional Admin pitch)** — all strategic framing must respect these boundaries, not focus only on the center.
2. **Basira routing** — the Regional Admin is the level that issues formal referrals to ministry HQ. Starting the approval chain at this level (not at the center) is the governance rule validated 2026-04-22.
3. **Ahmad's reporting line** — Ahmad (head of Quality at the center) reports up through the center director → the Assistant DG for Development Sector → the Director General.

## 13-regions scaling narrative

Saudi Arabia has 13 administrative regions (الرياض، مكة، المدينة، القصيم، الشرقية، عسير، تبوك، حائل، الحدود الشمالية، جازان، نجران، الباحة، الجوف). Each has its equivalent of this regional-admin structure. Document B's "1 → 13" scaling story rests on this — if the Al-Baha model works, it is replicable twelve more times.

- **The ministry HQ in Riyadh.** Minister + Assistant Ministers + Deputy Ministers (وكلاء). See [[mhrsd-leadership]].
