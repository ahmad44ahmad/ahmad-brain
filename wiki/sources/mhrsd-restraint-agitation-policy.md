# MHRSD Restraint & Psychological-Agitation Policy (سياسة التقييد)

## What this source is

The ministry's policy for **restraint (التقييد) and the management of psychological-agitation episodes (حالات الهياج
النفسي)** in residential social-care homes (الدور الاجتماعية الإيوائية) — including the comprehensive rehabilitation
centers. The policy is owned and monitored by **إدارة سلامة المستفيدين** (*Beneficiary Safety Administration*), the
ministry-level patient-safety body, under the General Admins for PwD support and elderly care.

The distilled artifact is the **minutes of a follow-up workshop** held **2025-08-05 (11/2/1447H)** over Webex — a
ministry-wide event (≈300 attendees: branch deputies, regional health-services coordinators, residential-home
directors, and the front-line staff who handle behavioural/psychiatric cases — medical-section supervisors, doctors,
psychologists, **PT + nursing specialists**, social-section supervisors). Ahmad's professional group (PT specialist +
quality/safety officer at the Al-Baha center) is squarely in the workshop's target audience; the folder sits in his
Drive. He is **not named in the workshop team** — treat this as the ministry doctrine he operates under, not direct
evidence of a personal role.

## The doctrine — minimise restraint, use alternatives, measure relentlessly

The policy's spine (workshop axes + recommendations):
- **Alternatives over restraint:** *"الاستفادة من وسائل الأمان بدل التقييد"* — use safety means instead of physical
  restraint, matched to the beneficiary's health state. Restraint is a last resort, not a routine tool.
- **Activate the psychologists:** *تفعيل دور الأخصائيين النفسيين* in the residential homes — behavioural expertise
  before/instead of restraint.
- **Precise monitoring + statistics:** apply the policy and the **approved forms**, with exact recording of every
  restraint case and a periodic statistical report (rate of change / improvement) escalated to إدارة سلامة المستفيدين.
- **Treatment-plan coordination with MoH:** update at-risk-beneficiary data + the treatment plan in coordination with
  Ministry-of-Health facilities (the psychiatric-care interface).
- **Escalation:** challenges in applying the policy are raised to إدارة سلامة المستفيدين.

## The evidence it works (hard numbers, 2025)

The workshop showcased two centers applying the policy guideline (دليل السياسات):
- **Comprehensive Rehab Center, Jazan** — restraint cases cut **76%** (29 → 7) over 5 months in 2025.
- **Comprehensive Rehab Center for Males, Kharj** — restraint cases cut **100%** (19 → 0) across 2025.

These are the ministry's own before/after figures — a documented case that the de-escalation-first model materially
reduces restraint, not just a policy aspiration.

## Why this matters for Basira

This is the **field-faithful source for Basira V4's deferred `RestraintRecord`** (PLAN §7, "RestraintRecord deferred
whole" — see [[basira-v4-requirements-ledger]]). When it is built, it should encode this policy's shape, not an
AI-invented one:
- a restraint **event** record tied to a beneficiary, with the alternative-measures-tried, the trigger, the duration,
  and the authorising clinician;
- a **monitoring/statistics** read (the rate-of-change report إدارة سلامة المستفيدين expects) — a natural
  [[basira-capability-domain-catalog]] behaviour-plan (PBS / NICE-NG11) interlock, with the **28-day mandatory review**
  that the empowerment instrument stack already specifies;
- the **psychology isolation/decision forms** (PC-01/02/03) flagged as missing-templates in [[mhrsd-rehab-center-forms]]
  are the data-entry instruments of this policy — sourcing them completes the loop.
