## Excellence framework — King Abdulaziz Quality Award

The doc adopts the **National Excellence Model = جائزة الملك عبدالعزيز للجودة** (King Abdulaziz Quality Award) as canonical, treating EFQM as a cross-reference peer. It enumerates the **9 excellence principles** and isolates **Criterion 4 — الشراكات والموارد (Partnerships & Resources)** as the doctrinal anchor for MHRSD's strategic-partnerships doctrine.

Frameworks named in the body: King Abdulaziz Quality Award · EFQM · ISO 9001 · ISO 22301 (BCP) · ISO 31000 (risk) · ISO 45001:2018 (OHS) · NFPA 101 Life Safety · CARF (international accreditation roadmap, **aspirational** — *"السعي للاعتماد الدولي"*).

**Not named:** MBNQA, Baldrige, the newer KAQA Saudi body — the "national model" in this doc = King Abdulaziz Quality Award only. SWOT appears as supporting strategic-planning method.

**Scope is bottom-up:** Al-Baha (case study) → MHRSD ("Golden Guide") → national model → CARF (2030 horizon).

## Innovation — two intertwined definitions

**(a) Ministry-level.** *الابتكار* = "producing something new or developing an existing thing," operationalised via the **Molham platform (منصة ملهم)** as an open idea-competition among ministry staff, paired with a full *Institutional Innovation Management Guide*. No explicit Design Thinking / Lean Startup / Stage-Gate naming — methodology framed as: open competition → evaluation → prototyping → ministry-wide deployment, with partnership-governance wrapping.

**(b) University-level.** Innovation = research commercialisation (Jeddah University Deanship of Research & Innovation; incubators + accelerators including *حاضنة كونتينر* / Container; patent filings; conversion of research into economic products). The title's *منظومات البحث العلمي* (scientific research systems) maps almost entirely to this content.

⚠️ **Jeddah University vs Al-Baha University discrepancy.** Jeddah University appears repeatedly as the "academic complement," but Al-Baha center's actual academic partnership is with Al-Baha University. The Jeddah framing likely came from the source corpus, not from an actual Al-Baha-center collaboration. Verify before citing as a partnership.

## أنسنة الخدمات التقنية — the design-principle register

This is the doc's most distinctive lexical contribution. Two operational meanings:

**1. Dual-client safety (السلامة ثنائية العميل).** Beneficiary safety and staff safety are organically co-dependent — a staff member injured by a beneficiary's behavioural crisis will burn out and under-care. Humanising technical services means designing for *both* sides simultaneously. Reframes safety as a symmetric human relation, not a one-way duty-of-care.

**2. خوارزمية الإحسان (Algorithm of Ihsan / Benevolence) — load-bearing coinage.** The doc describes Basira as embedding an explicit "humanisation-of-technology" layer via this algorithm: it reminds caregivers of each beneficiary's fine-grained personal preferences (food colour, sensory tolerances, family-call schedule) to preserve dignity and reduce cognitive load on staff. **Naming note (resolved 2026-04-25):** Ahmad has since upgraded the user-facing name of this feature from *خوارزمية الإحسان* to *ملف الكرامة* (Dignity File, see [[999-zero-paper-master]]) — same feature, more accurate name (*الكرامة* = inherent dignity vs *إحسان* = asymmetric benevolence). Quotations from this F-5.1 doc are preserved verbatim with the historical name; current/forward writing should use *ملف الكرامة*.

Both phrases extend [[empowerment-vocabulary]] with a fourth register beyond critique/vision/procedural: a **design-principle** register naming patterns for technology built around dignity. They are added to the lexicon as part of this distillation.

Specific humanisation design patterns named in the doc: sensory-reducing architecture (prevent behavioural crises), Personal Emergency Evacuation Plans (PEEPs), strobe lights (hearing-impaired), low-frequency audible alarms, Braille wayfinding, RFID rapid-evacuation tracking, NFPA 101 Life Safety adapted for disability.

- Open-data policy as infrastructure for open innovation.
- Bridge to Al-Baha University via digital transformation initiatives.

**Not named:** IRB / ethics-committee infrastructure. KSAU-HS or King Saud University. The research-partnership narrative is Jeddah + Al-Baha University, not the medical-university axis.
