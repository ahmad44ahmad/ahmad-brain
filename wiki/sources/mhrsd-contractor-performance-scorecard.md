# MHRSD Contractor Performance Scorecard

## What this is (reframe)

The "fourth KPI track" surfaced in [[mhrsd-2025-social-operational-plan-albaha]] (بطاقة مؤشر الأداء — قسم التشغيل والصيانة) is **not** an internal-staff KPI track. It's a **vendor SLA evaluation form** scoring the *outsourced contractor* who provides cleaning, personal-care, laundry, kitchen, and maintenance services across MHRSD rehabilitation centers + social-care homes.

This explains why the operational plan's standard 8-section template lists "آلية تشغيل ومتابعة عقود العناية الشخصية والصحية والصيانة والنظافة وفق ما نصت عليه كراسة الشروط والمواصفات" as a required section — the contracts are core operational reality, and this scorecard is how performance under those contracts gets measured.

## Structure (1000-point monthly evaluation)

| Service domain | Points | Item count | What it covers |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| النظافة (Cleaning) | 285 | 27 | Building/floor/furniture/bathroom cleaning, pest control, disinfection, materials, labor |
| العناية الشخصية (Personal care) | 265 | 22 | Beneficiary hygiene, oral care, hair/nail care, clothing/bedding, PT/OT session execution, materials |
| الغسيل والمطبخ (Laundry + kitchen) | 225 | 19 | Laundry quality, food sterilization, meal preparation + delivery, kitchen hygiene |
| الصيانة (Maintenance) | 225 | 23 | Electrical, HVAC, plumbing, building, elevators, copiers, preventive maintenance plans |
| **Total** | **1000** | **91** | |

**Score = (achieved points / 1000) × 100.**

## Recurring sub-structure within each domain

Every one of the 4 domains follows the same pattern:

1. **Service-quality items** — high points each (10-20). Tied to specific tracking logs (سجلات المتابعة اليومية / الدورية).
2. **Form-discipline item** — 10-20 points. "انتظام استخدام النماذج Log Sheet + Checklist يوميا."
3. **Headcount-vs-contract item** — 10-20 points. "نقص العمالة حسب ما هو مقرر بالأعداد بالعقد."
4. **Labor-block** — 8 items × 5 points each (40 points), uniform across domains:
   - Attendance + sign-in
   - Performance + discipline
   - Uniform + ID + health certificate
   - Personal hygiene
   - Use of safety tools + methods
   - Understanding of assigned tasks
   - Emergency response outside hours
   - Response to on-the-job training
5. **Materials/tools items** — 5-15 points each. Availability, quality, distribution, training on use, no incidents.

The repeating labor block (40 points × 4 domains = 160 points / 1000 = 16%) means **labor performance is 16% of the contractor's monthly score**, mostly through soft factors (attendance, uniform, training response).

## Escalation rule

> Below-90% performance on any single domain triggers a **محضر تدني** (under-performance memorandum) prepared by the branch committee detailing per-point deduction reasons + an improvement plan from the contractor.

The branch committee signs the evaluation:
- مدير الفرع (branch director)
- 2 × أعضاء (branch members)
- مندوب المقاول (contractor representative)

The official **ختم** (stamp) is required when score <90% — institutional record of vendor underperformance.

## Why this matters operationally

**For Ahmad as Quality head:** every month, the Quality department signs off on this 91-item evaluation alongside the other branch members. Branch-internal Quality is the discipline that makes the deductions defensible if the contractor disputes them.

**For Basira as a quality-driven digital transformation:**
- This scorecard is **already paper-based + monthly** — clear digital-transformation candidate.
- Linking the per-domain logs (سجلات المتابعة اليومية / الدورية) to a single dashboard would automate the 285+265+225+225 = 1000-point calculation.
- The scorecard's structure mirrors the medical-side scorecard ([[mhrsd-2025-kpi-master-scorecard]]) — additive weighted sum, threshold-triggered escalation, branch-committee accountability.
- **Contractor underperformance = missed kingdom-wide standard, not local complaint.** Documenting the gap monthly creates a regulatory-grade audit trail.

## Cross-link to PT (Ahmad's department)

One PT-relevant item sits in the Personal Care domain:

> "تنفيذ جلسات العلاج الطبيعي والوظيفي بشكل منتظم حسب توجيهات المشرف على القسم — 20 points"

→ Contractor is evaluated on **execution** of PT/OT sessions per department-supervisor instructions. Ahmad's role as PT supervisor directly determines whether this 20-point item earns full marks. **PT supervisor sign-off is contract-relevant.**

| Track (per ops plan) | What it measures | Vault distillation |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Social | Center social-services performance | [[mhrsd-2024-kpi-social-section]] |
| 2. Beneficiary safety (medical-side) | Per-indicator clinical safety | [[mhrsd-2026-kpi-health-social-homes]] |

| 4. **Operations + Maintenance** | **Outsourced contractor SLA** | **This page** |

Tracks 1-3 measure *staff* performance. Track 4 measures *vendor* performance. The structural distinction matters: tracks 1-3 are individual-accountability scorecards; track 4 is contract-enforcement instrument.

## Tracking-log dependencies

The scorecard repeatedly refers to two tracking-log types:

- **سجلات المتابعة اليومية** (daily monitoring logs) — for high-frequency items like cleaning, personal hygiene, meal delivery
- **سجلات المتابعة الدورية** (periodic monitoring logs) — for lower-frequency items like furniture, pest control, plumbing fixtures

These logs are the **upstream evidence base** for the monthly score. Centers that don't keep good logs can't defend deductions if the contractor disputes them. **Log discipline = scorecard defensibility.**
