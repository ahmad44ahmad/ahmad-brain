# 999 — Disability Care + Empowerment Strategy (Portfolio)

## The document is misleadingly titled

The Drive filename (`999 مركز التأهيل الشامل موضوع شامل 500 صفحة`) names "the comprehensive rehabilitation center" and "500 pages" — both misleading.

- **True subject:** the **national Saudi disability care + empowerment system** at ministry scale. The center is a test bed, not the unit of analysis.
- **True shape:** not one monograph but **seven stitched strategic documents** across ~1.5M characters. Treat as a portfolio that Ahmad uses as his thinking reservoir.

**Opening title inside the doc:** *«منظومة رعاية وتمكين الأشخاص ذوي الإعاقة في السعودية: رؤية استراتيجية»* — "The Disability Care and Empowerment System in Saudi Arabia: A Strategic Vision."

Doc A's Part Two lists **8 strategic axes**: governance · service quality · empowerment · family · cadres · community inclusion · technology · sustainable finance.

Doc F's Part Three lists **5 strategic goals**: health/rehab quality · inclusive education · economic empowerment · community-care transition · universal access.

Doc E is the most original. Its structure in four parts: (1) strategic-transformation statement + new vision; (2) designing the "social operating system"; (3) engineering impact-centered governance and contracting; (4) implementation roadmap + pilot.

## Ahmad's signature frames (coined or anchored in this doc)

- **«مختبرات الممكن البشري»** (Human Potential Labs) — proposed institutional rebrand replacing "comprehensive rehabilitation center."
- **«التوأم الرقمي للأثر الاجتماعي»** (Digital Twin for Social Impact) — simulation-driven pre-allocation of interventions + outcome verification.
- **«نظام التشغيل الاجتماعي»** (Social Operating System) — meta-frame for the redesign.
- **«مهندس مسارات حياة»** (Life Path Architect) — proposed title for the front-line worker.
- **«قائد منظومة التمكين»** (Ecosystem Enablement Lead) — proposed title for the center director.
- **«المستثمر في الأثر الاجتماعي»** (Social Impact Investor) — proposed framing for senior leadership.
- **«دستور التمكين»** (Empowerment Manifesto) — five-point internal charter Doc E proposes.
- **«الشجاعة المؤسسية»** (Institutional Courage) — closing demand.
- **«من طغيان الكم إلى حوكمة الأثر»** (from quantity tyranny to impact governance) — KPI-replacement slogan.
- **«اللوائح الدفاعية»** (defensive regulations) — diagnostic term for over-restrictive central rule-making.

Contrast pivots:

- **رعاية vs تمكين** — legacy-failure vs only-legitimate-goal framing.
- **النموذج الطبي vs النموذج الاجتماعي** — medical/needs model is the failure being dismantled.
- **المؤسسات الكلية vs الدمج المجتمعي** — de-institutionalization (Goffman-derived critique).
- **مؤشرات بيروقراطية vs مؤشرات الأثر** — bureaucratic vs impact KPIs.

The pivot from رعاية to تمكين is the single through-line of the portfolio. This is the intellectual spine of both [[basira]] and the [[social-handicap-compass]] — when Ahmad writes about those projects, the vocabulary here is the origin.

## Named initiatives (Doc A/B)

1. *«صوتك مسموع ومؤثر»* — beneficiary voice + choice mechanisms.
2. *«كوادر مؤهلة لرعاية إنسانية»* — workforce ethics + competence reform.
3. *«أسرتي سندي: دعم وتمكين متكامل»* — family as care + empowerment partner.
4. *«بياناتنا أساس تخطيطنا»* — national PWD data infrastructure (addresses the "no national database" gap).
5. *«مدينتي دامجة»* — universal-design pilot city.
6. *«عقول متفتحة لمجتمع دامج»* — cultural/awareness transformation.

**Signature pilot:** *«واحة التمكين»* — Empowerment Oasis. 18 months, 3 phases (0–6 / 6–12 / 12–18 months). Operationalises the Human Potential Labs paradigm at one test site before scale.

## Load-bearing statistics and numeric facts

- **84%** of individuals in rehab centres lack personal emergency-preparedness plans. Directly motivates the PEEP rollout.
- **7–10%** of Saudi Arabia's population are PWD.
- **Article 28:** employers with ≥25 workers must employ ≥4% qualified PWD.
- **Ramp inclination:** no steeper than **1:12 (8%)**. An alternative passage cites **≤6%**.
- **100%** target on preventive-vaccination compliance.
- **<2 per 1000 inpatient days** — target for bed-sore (pressure ulcer) incidence.
- **50%** efficiency gain cited in the "Unified Standard Identity" precedent for digital transformation.
- **Pay-for-Success trigger:** payment only when Digital Twin metrics verify **≥50%** of beneficiaries have transitioned to a higher independence level.
- **Supreme Council report** goes directly to the **Prime Minister** annually.
- **Doc D source base:** 172 sources on rehab-centre governance.
- **واحة التمكين pilot:** 18 months, 3 phases.
- **OSH roadmap:** 24 months, 3 phases (0–6 / 7–12 / 13–24).
- **Access minimum:** corridor / door / passage width **≥90 cm**.

## What the doc's stance actually is (for each live tension)

| Tension | Doc's position |
|---|---|
| Medical vs social model | Decisively social / capabilities. Medical/needs frame is the failure being dismantled. |
| رعاية vs تمكين | Welfare is legacy failure; empowerment is the only legitimate goal. Care is means, not end. |
| Central vs regional authority | Anti-centralised "defensive bylaws"; pro field-tested protocols + regional pilots + bottom-up incident reporting (OVR). |
| Quantity vs impact KPIs | Explicit war on "عدد الخدمات المقدمة"; replacement = degree of independence + active contribution. |
| Institutional vs community care | Pro de-institutionalization; "total institutions" produce isolation and dependency by design. |
| Compliance vs innovation procurement | Argues for Pay-for-Success / outcome-based in place of input-based privatisation. |
| Defensive risk-aversion vs dignity | Restrictive safety-justified rules (e.g. clothing, restraints) are dignity violations; "integrated safety system" reconciles both. |
| Blame-the-worker vs systemic accountability | Rejects blame-the-worker culture; mandates RCA and systemic analysis. |
| Special-ed graduates doing basic care | Framed as misuse of qualifications; redeploy them to planning, program design, family counseling. |

## How to use this source

- **For any Basira pitch or narrative:** the vocabulary here is the origin. Pull phrases like *«من الرعاية إلى التمكين»*, *«التوأم الرقمي للأثر الاجتماعي»*, *«مختبرات الممكن البشري»* when you need Ahmad's authentic voice.
- **For PT modeling:** Doc G (OSH) and Doc I (TQM) are the most relevant. The TQM vocabulary (FOCUS-PDSA, Kaizen, OVR, RCA) is where Ahmad's Quality-track expertise lives. But note: Ahmad's [[pt-modeling]] rules bar personal initiatives (Basira, Tamkeen, Masarrah) from the official PT modelling document. TQM framework mentions are OK; initiative names are not.
- **For disability-rights narratives:** Article 28 + CRPD + 4% quota + 7-10% population + 84% PEEP gap form the core evidence stack.
- **For social-handicap-compass framing ([[social-handicap-compass]]):** Doc E is the full articulation of "Ahmad works social layer, not medical." It is the analytical source that CBAHI/JCI are correctly excluded from his work.

## What the doc is NOT

- Not a single 500-page strategic document despite the filename.
- Not narrative-only and not prescriptive-only — moves between dialogic (B, C), prescriptive (A, F, G, I), operational (H), and visionary/persuasive (E).
- Not CBAHI-aligned by choice — it deliberately uses TQM / EFQM / PDSA / RACI / Balanced Scorecard vocabulary, bypassing the healthcare-accreditation lens. Consistent with Ahmad's PT-modeling scope rules.
- Not a center-level operational plan — unit of analysis is the national system; the center is the test bed.
- Not finalized policy — Ahmad's thinking substrate, intellectual reservoir feeding Basira and the PT-modeling deliverables. Not submission-ready.
- Not free of repetition — several passages (7-10% population, 4% quota, 100% vaccination KPI, SLP-licensure block) recur across the stitched docs because each is self-contained.
