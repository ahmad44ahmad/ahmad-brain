## Contents — three pillars

### Pillar 1 — Legal/administrative challenge over Cabinet Decision No. 142

Ahmad's folder contains a detailed legal analysis of **قرار مجلس الوزراء رقم 142** (Council of Ministers Decision 142), dated **09/02/1446 AH** (2024-08-23 CE), concerning the continuation of overtime-hours allowance for certain health-sector positions. Ahmad's analysis systematically argues why the decision does NOT apply to employees of Comprehensive Rehabilitation Centers, across four critical axes:

| # | Argument | Supporting files |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Establishment type** — the decision explicitly requires the position to be in "a hospital, health center, or laboratory". Rehab centers, under their current and historical classification inside MHRSD (MOSA / MLSD / HRSD), are categorised as **social entities** (كيانات اجتماعية), not exclusively health facilities. | 18, 19 |
| 2 | **Organisational reporting line** — Decision targets the health sector. Rehab centers historically report to social ministries (MOSA → MLSD → HRSD, see [[mhrsd-era-timeline]]), placing them outside the direct scope of Ministry-of-Health regulatory/financial decisions. | 16, 17 |
| 3 | **Fiscal allocation** — Decision's Article 4 explicitly binds allowance costs to ceilings within **Ministry of Health's budget**. This fiscal specificity reinforces applicability exclusively to MoH-funded entities. | 22 |
| 4 | **Excluded positions** — the decision also names specific health-sector roles not covered by the Health Positions Regulation (e.g., administrative assistant, medical-statistics technician) with fixed-amount schedules — further evidence of a strictly health-sector framing. | 4 |
