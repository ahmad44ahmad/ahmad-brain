# Family-Fear Barrier

## Why this note exists

The vault already holds family-guidance as an org-mandate (الإرشاد الأسري in [[community-integration-project]]), the «فخ الإعانة» benefit-trap as vocabulary ([[empowerment-vocabulary]]), and the B6/B7 fear slots on the dial ([[social-handicap-compass]]). What was missing is the **structured psychology of family resistance** as the demand-side blocker behind the ~2% transition rate (KPI ت-1-1, 2019 baseline, [[community-integration-project]]). This is that model.

The load-bearing reframe: **family consent = shared responsibility, not a gate the family unilaterally fails.** The fear is the barrier — and a recorded fear must open an intervention, never auto-route a person to STAY.

## The three sub-fears → their interventions

1. **Knowledge gap** — "I don't know how to care for him, or what he is even capable of." → **family psychoeducation + peer/family-to-family mentoring** `[verify]` (family psychoeducation in resource-poor settings reported feasible, single source). The capability frame ([[basira-capability-domain-catalog]]) is the answer to "what is he capable of."
2. **Financial worry** — "I can't afford it." → **benefit-linkage**: the alternative-services + entitlements catalogue ([[alternative-services-catalogue]]) is the structural answer. Anchored in Saudi-Law entitlements — social support Art. 15 `[verified]`, assistive devices Art. 16 `[verified]` (see [[saudi-disability-rights-framework]]).
3. **"Who cares for him after I die?"** (the deepest, most-cited) — → **future-care planning**: the #1 caregiver worry is the future `[verified]`; **55% of families are "not ready or unwilling" to plan** `[verified]`. Named instruments: 7 future-care-planning domains, Letter of Intent, circles of support, special-needs trusts `[verified]`; a structured legal/financial training intervention significantly increased concrete planning AND reduced caregiver burden `[verified]`.

## Cross-cutting governing principle

**Supported decision-making (CRPD Art. 12 + General Comment No. 1)** `[verified]` dismantles the "he can't decide so he must stay" objection — the family/circle supports the person's own will, not substitute guardianship. The community-living right itself is **CRPD Art. 19 + General Comment No. 5 + the 2022 DI Guidelines** `[verified]` (see [[saudi-disability-rights-framework]]).

## The causal root and the anti-pattern

«فخ الإعانة» (the **benefit-trap**) is the economic root under sub-fear #2: tying support to dependency makes exit a financial loss for the family. **Anti-pattern (hard rule): aid must NOT be conditioned on discharge** ("الإسقاط… لحصولها على المساعدات" — closing the file to obtain the aid). That perverse incentive ties benefit to file-closure rather than to need. The fix inverts it — benefit-linkage *removes* the financial fear instead of weaponizing it. A recorded family-fear barrier opens a required intervention plan; it can never become a resting STAY justification.
