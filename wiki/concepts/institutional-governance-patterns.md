# Institutional Governance Patterns

## What this note carries

Four cross-cutting governance rules surfaced from the MHRSD final harvest (self-assessment form, ISO risk matrix, KPI cards). Each is an **anti-gaming or accountability lever** — a small structural rule that changes incentives at scale. They are extracted as reusable institutional-design patterns, distinct from any single form. All Arabic field detail stays in the source library; this note is the English pattern. (Sources carry real staff names in owner columns — mapped here to **roles only**; the confidential sovereign material in the same folder is internalised as silent constraint and **never cited**.)

## 1. N/A-governance (the out-of-control criterion)

A criterion that is **outside the center's control never scores 0**. The rule, verbatim from the self-assessment instrument: **«البند الذي لا ينطبق على الفرع يعطى الدرجة كاملة»** — an inapplicable item gets the full score. But the lever is the second half: a full score for an N/A item **triggers a mandatory escalation to the responsible central agency**. The branch is not penalised for a gap it cannot close; the gap is routed upward as an accountability item.

This is the paper origin of Basira's **AccountabilityGap** escalation ([[basira-v4-engines-and-corpus]]): "service unavailable in region" is a system property to fix, not a defect of the branch or the beneficiary — the same inversion [[empowerment-ledger-vs-custodial-return]] applies to funding.

## 2. Strategic over-achievement (kpiClass)

Two classes of KPI, scored asymmetrically:

- **Strategic KPIs** (quality, empowerment, partnership) — **reward over-achievement above 100%**, up to a **125% ceiling**. Doing more than the target *should* register.
- **Quantitative / gameable KPIs** (raw counts) — **cap at 100%**. Rewarding over-achievement on a count invites gaming (inflate the denominator, pad the activity).

The KPI-card schema this rests on (objective / indicator / code / dimension / owner / formula / unit / frequency / baseline / polarity / target) and the **«الآلية القياسية»** roll-up — where weighted operational sub-indicators sum to a 100%/period headline — are documented in [[mhrsd-2025-kpi-portfolio]]; this rule is the *governance* layer over that schema. The 100% cap on gameable indicators is Ahmad's directive and authoritative. The named social-empowerment indicators (including after-care return-to-family) submit up a center → branch → ministry swimlane.

## 3. Plan-B / business-continuity doctrine

A risk that **cannot be structurally removed still needs a named external-partner continuity plan** — Civil Defense, Red Crescent, MoH, the Emirate. The reasoning case is the Al-Baha fire-risk reverse-engineering: where the hazard can't be engineered out, the answer is a named partner and a written continuity plan, not a residual-risk acceptance. This sits in the ISO risk matrix's BCP rows ([[999-albaha-qms-bcp]]). It converts "we can't fix this" into "here is who we call and what happens."

## 4. Anti-neglect shift design

The #1 barrier families name is the **feeling of neglect**. The structural answer is in the shift design: **mornings = specialists doing the heavy clinical/admin load; evenings and nights = recreational and social** — staffed and programmed as social time, not as after-hours custody. Designing the night shift as social rather than administrative is a governance choice that directly attacks the neglect signal.

## Why this matters

These are the institutional-design rules the Basira engine layer encodes ([[basira-v4-engines-and-corpus]]) and the governance spine behind the «الخيط الذهبي» Golden-Thread argument ([[al-baha-quality-project-2024]]): compliance and excellence are in service of empowerment, not of facility-control density. They pair with the disposition sort in [[case-shifting-de-dilution]] and the funding inversion in [[empowerment-ledger-vs-custodial-return]]. The supervisory-visit/self-assessment instrument that scores them is in [[mhrsd-supervisory-visit-instruments]].
