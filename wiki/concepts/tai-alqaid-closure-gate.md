# طي القيد Closure Gate

## What this note carries

[[training-development-project]] names the «طي القيد» steering committee and Circular #116794 and the 3-month window — but only as a single committee. The genuine delta is that closure is gated by **two committees in sequence**, with a named dossier and hard family-facing preconditions. This is the closure-governance model: the documentary mirror of community integration, and the «طي القيد» counterpart at the per-case scale to the population-level sort in [[case-shifting-de-dilution]].

## The two-committee sequence

Closure is **not** a single approval — it is verify → approve across two distinct committees:

1. **اللجنة الفنية (Technical committee, قرار 117768) — VERIFIES.** It checks the closure dossier for completeness and correctness. It does not close the file.
2. **اللجنة التوجيهية (Steering committee, قرار 117224) — APPROVES.** Only the Steering committee can approve ending طي القيد. **No file closure happens before Steering approval.**

**The linchpin:** the **مدير إدارة التأهيل الاجتماعي** sits on *both* committees — نائب رئيس on the Technical, أمين on the Steering — carrying the verified file from "verified" to "approved." One role bridges the two gates.

## The closure dossier — تعميم 117792 (أ–ح)

The dossier the Technical committee verifies, eight components:

- (أ–ج) **medical, social, and psychological reports**;
- **دراسة الحالة المستفيضة للأسرة** (in-depth family case study) + **البحث الاجتماعي للأسرة** (family social research);
- **alternative services arranged** (the routing menu in [[alternative-services-catalogue]] is in place, not promised);
- **an after-care plan**;
- **(117792-د) the complete report delivered to the family BEFORE closure** — a hard precondition, not a courtesy;
- **family acceptance** + **(117792-ز) receipt of the beneficiary** by the family.

Report-to-family-before-closure and family receipt are **load-bearing gates**: closure cannot complete without the family having the full report in hand and formally receiving the person.

> Note: the precise composition of the checklist — and whether report-to-family + family-receipt are modeled as hard gates with the Steering committee as the sole final approver — is documented in the design dossier and pending a confirmation pass with the center. Treated here as the working model.

## The social-model invariant

The gate governs **closure of the file**, never the person's life. **Moving home is NEVER blocked.** When a precondition is unmet, the framing is **«حاجزٌ يُفكَّك، لا حكمٌ بعدم الأهلية»** — a barrier to dismantle, never a verdict of ineligibility — the same disqualifier-as-barrier discipline as [[integration-readiness-classification]]. A beneficiary can go home while the file remains open; the gate is administrative, the right is not gated.

## Re-admission is a separate channel

**إعادة القيد (re-admission)** does **not** route back through the two طي-قيد committees. It goes through a **separate central committee (تعميم 179291)** — a distinct channel. Re-opening a closed case is governed centrally, not by reversing the local closure gate.

## After-care (الرعاية اللاحقة) `[UNVERIFIED]`

Post-closure, an after-care follow-up loop runs over a **6-month → 1-year window** `[UNVERIFIED — Ahmad's operational ground truth, not stated in the framework regulation]`, staffed by the same nine-discipline care team. A relapse **re-opens the barrier** rather than failing the person — consistent with the social-model invariant. The window figure is flagged because it is operational practice, not codified in the source regulation.

## Why this matters

This is the keystone that makes empowerment auditable: closure is a *gated, evidenced, family-consented* event, not a clerk's stroke. It deepens [[training-development-project]] (which only named the Steering committee), connects to the funding inversion ([[empowerment-ledger-vs-custodial-return]]) and the demand-side family-fear work ([[family-fear-barrier]]), and is the per-case mechanism the Basira empowerment model encodes ([[basira-v4-disability-empowerment-model]]).
