# Case-Shifting & De-Dilution

## What this note carries — and how it differs from readiness scoring

This is the **system-level disposition sort**: the rule for *which population goes where* that makes "closing custodial centers" an operable move rather than a slogan. It is distinct from [[integration-readiness-classification]] — that note scores an **individual's** readiness and frames the disqualifier as a barrier-to-dismantle; **this note is the population-level two-way sort** that, applied across a center's roster, lets the center change what it *is*. Readiness = scoring one person; de-dilution = re-sorting the whole population so an institution can be retired.

## The two-way sort

A center's mixed population is sorted in two directions:

- **OUT → excellence / community tracks** — beneficiaries who are **empowerment-capable and have a family or support circle**. They route into day-care, vocational, home-care, and the alternative-services menu (the tracks in [[basira-v4-disability-empowerment-model]]).
- **→ dedicated long-term medical care** — **total-chronic, no-cognition, no-family** cases whose needs are genuinely medical and continuous. They route to a dedicated long-term medical facility, not a mixed custodial center.

## Why de-dilution is the unlock

A center that mixes both populations can never stop being custodial — the chronic-medical cohort anchors its identity and budget to the institutional model. **De-dilution** (de-mixing the population) is therefore the precondition for change: once the empowerment-capable cohort has routed out and the medical cohort has a dedicated home, the custodial center has no population left to justify itself — it can be **closed or repurposed**. This is the operational meaning of de-institutionalization that the directive's "de-dilution goal" names, built on the community-integration guide's 5-class / 7-step routing logic ([[community-integration-project]]).

## The load-bearing guard (do not violate)

The sort uses capability/severity information — but **IQ and Vineland adaptive-behaviour bands SIZE the support package; they NEVER GATE the right** to community living. A low band means *more support*, not *stays institutionalised*. This is the Hub's anti-eligibility-gate amendment to the medical model, and it is grounded in **CRPD Art. 19** (community living as a right, not a reward for a test score) — see [[saudi-disability-rights-framework]]. The disqualifier is always a barrier to dismantle, never a verdict of ineligibility ([[integration-readiness-classification]]).

## Why this matters

De-dilution is where the governance levers ([[institutional-governance-patterns]]) and the readiness scoring meet a concrete institutional outcome: a population re-sorted, a custodial center retired, the budget following the person out rather than holding them in ([[empowerment-ledger-vs-custodial-return]] for the funding side). It is the structural mechanism behind «طي القيد» at the population scale, where [[integration-readiness-classification]] is the per-case gate.
