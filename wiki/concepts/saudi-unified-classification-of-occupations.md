# Saudi Unified Classification of Occupations

## What this concept carries

[[hrsd-pms]] documents the performance-appraisal machinery and notes that the behavioural-competency pillar is *built on* the Saudi Unified Classification of Occupations — but it does not unpack that backbone. This note is that standalone framework: generic, reusable civil-service knowledge that outlives any one appraisal cycle. It is distilled from the clean English extract of the 129 KB `دليل تطبيق التصنيف السعودي للمهن` (the only genuinely under-represented, PII-free knowledge in the PMS harvest); the live-PII raw files in that harvest are excluded from the vault and kept local.

## The occupation hierarchy (5 tiers)

A nested classification, aligned to **ISCO-08** (occupations) and **ISCED-11** (education levels):

| Tier | Arabic | Count |
|---|---|---|
| Major group | المجموعة الرئيسية | 10 |
| Sub-major | المجموعة الفرعية | 43 |
| Minor | المجموعة الثانوية | 130 |
| Unit | مجموعة الوحدات | 432 |
| Occupation | المهنة | 2013 |

Every government occupation resolves to one leaf in this tree; the leaf carries the occupation's required competency profile.

## The competency families

Two top-level families, the structure the appraisal's «الجدارات» pillar reads:

- **Behavioural competencies (الجدارات السلوكية)** — split into **general** (every employee) and **leadership** (supervisory/management roles).
- **Technical competencies (الجدارات الفنية)** — occupation-specific skills.

For a given occupation, the framework prescribes *which* competencies apply and *at what level* — which is exactly what the manager rates in [[hrsd-pms]] (e.g., for `أخصائي علاج طبيعي`: المسؤولية، العمل الجماعي، المرونة للتغيير، المبادرة، كفاءة الإنفاق).

## The 5-level mastery scale + the grade cascade

Each competency is rated on a **5-level mastery scale (مستوى الإتقان)**, ascending. The **required level rises with the salary grade**: the same competency demands a higher mastery rung as the employee climbs. The progression is named by a **title ladder** — مساعد → ممارس → … → خبير → مستشار (assistant → practitioner → … → expert → consultant) — so the classification doubles as a career-progression map, not just a job-coding scheme.

## Why this matters

This is the structural substrate under the whole HRSD appraisal ([[hrsd-pms]]) and under any career-progression argument: it defines what competencies a role requires, at what level, and how that level escalates toward the expert/consultant tiers. For the Kingdom-level PT-supervisor nomination narrative ([[hrsd-work]], [[ahmad-career-arc]]), it is the framework that makes "operating above the credential-level competency band" a defined, classification-anchored claim rather than an assertion.
